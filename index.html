What is gruepr?

gruepr is a free and open-source application for placing students in optimal project teams. A student survey collects information from the students, and then gruepr finds the best set of teams.

gruepr is very flexible: the instructor can include in the survey whatever questions they would like to use to help form teams, and the algorithm can use the survey data in a variety of instructor-chosen ways.

gruepr keeps data private: no information from or about students is ever sent to gruepr. The survey is typically a Google Form that lives on Google Drive, but could also be performed using any preferred survey instrument.

gruepr was created by an educator for fellow educators: it will remain always free of cost, free from ads, and free from selling of any data. More information on how gruepr works can be found in this open access article published in the ASEE Computers in Education Journal. How to use Surveymaker!

1. Once you open the app, choose SurveyMaker from the main screen to start.


2. Fill in Survey Title and choose which questions you want to include in your survey. (First/Last Name, Email Address, Gender, Race)


3. Customize your attribute questions and choose the matching response by using the dropdown menu. You can choose to have from 0 to 15 questions in your survey, and you can choose a custom response if none of the given options match.


4. Choose whether you want to include a timezone option and allow students to enter the times when they are either free or busy.


5. Allow students to specify which section of the class they are in.


6. Allow students to declare preferred or non-preferred teammates within the class.

Once you have completed your survey in SurveyMaker, click on the “Create the current survey as: Google form.”

Congrats! All of your survey options have been consolidated into a single Google Form that you can send out to your students and allow them to fill out.

How to use gruepr

1. Choose gruepr from the main screen.


2. First, import your survey data.


3. Make sure all the columns and categories line up, then click OK.


4. Select which class sections you would like to make teams for, as well as team size. If the team size does not fit evenly into the total number of students, gruepr will do the math for you!


5. Choose to prevent certain groups of students from being isolated in the teams created.


6. Specify the weight of each attribute question out of 10 to be considered in the optimal creation of teams. Also choose whether you want to require these attributes to be within each team or if there are attributes that cannot be on the same team.


7. Choose the desired number of hours you would like each team to ideally meet throughout the week. Also choose the weight of this attribute out of 10.


8. Here you can specify requested or prevented teammates, which ensures that certain students will be on the same team or NOT on the same team.


9. Once you are finished, click the “Create Teams” button.


Congratulations! You are finished and gruepr has provided you with the optimal teams based on your selections. View the teams created and choose how to export them if you wish.
